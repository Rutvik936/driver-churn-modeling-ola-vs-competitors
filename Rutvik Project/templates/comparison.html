1. Ola Attrition Analysis

With the lowest attrition rate at 18% among the three platforms, Ola demonstrates relatively better driver retention. The primary reason for attrition is relocation, suggesting that many drivers leave when moving to new cities rather than due to platform-specific issues. This could indicate that Ola provides satisfactory working conditions, but may need to improve inter-city transfer policies to retain relocating drivers.

2. Uber Attrition Analysis

Uber shows a moderate attrition rate of 34%, with rating pressure being the dominant concern. This suggests that Uber's driver rating system creates significant stress for drivers, potentially leading to burnout. The platform might benefit from revising its rating algorithms or providing more support for drivers facing unfair ratings. The data indicates that while Uber remains popular, its quality control mechanisms may be too stringent.

3. Rapido Attrition Analysis

Rapido has the highest attrition rate at 38%, primarily due to low order volume. This indicates that while drivers may be satisfied with the platform itself, they're leaving because they can't get enough rides to make it financially viable. Rapido should focus on increasing customer acquisition and optimizing ride allocation algorithms. The data suggests Rapido's main challenge is market penetration rather than driver satisfaction with platform policies.
